## 2. Content Pillars and Themes

BOCC's Facebook content should rotate across five pillars. Each pillar maps to a strategic goal.

### Pillar 1: The Tuesday Invite (40% of posts)
**Goal:** Drive attendance to the weekly event
**Tone:** Warm, welcoming, low-pressure
**Examples:**
- Tuesday morning reminder posts
- "See you tomorrow" Monday posts
- Recap posts after the event
- First-timer spotlights
- "What to expect at your first BOCC" explainers

### Pillar 2: Community Spotlight (25% of posts)
**Goal:** Celebrate members, build emotional connection, create shareable content
**Tone:** Proud, personal, story-driven
**Examples:**
- Attendee features: "Meet [Name], who started coming to BOCC 6 months ago and just launched..."
- Testimonial reshares (repurpose the LinkedIn testimonials from Scott, Yelenna, Lena, Jennifer, Benjamin, Freed)
- "Connection stories" -- two people who met at BOCC and now work together
- Milestone celebrations: "This week marks [Name]'s 50th BOCC Tuesday"

### Pillar 3: Afternoon Edition and Special Events (15% of posts)
**Goal:** Promote BOCC Afternoon Edition, grow the second format
**Tone:** Casual, exploratory, fun
**Examples:**
- Venue previews for upcoming Afternoon Edition locations
- "Can't make Tuesday mornings? Here's your alternative"
- Recaps with photos from Afternoon Edition events
- Venue partner tags (Filled with Love Buffalo Cafe, Fitz Books & Waffles, Meet & Eat Charcuterie)

### Pillar 4: Buffalo Entrepreneurial Ecosystem (10% of posts)
**Goal:** Position BOCC as the hub of Buffalo's startup community
**Tone:** Informative, connected, generous
**Examples:**
- Share relevant Buffalo business news
- Highlight other local events and resources (without competing -- amplify the ecosystem)
- Seasonal content: "Buffalo startup scene in spring" roundups
- BootSector updates

### Pillar 5: Behind the Scenes and Culture (10% of posts)
**Goal:** Humanize the organization, build affinity
**Tone:** Authentic, casual, sometimes humorous
**Examples:**
- Zack setting up at 7 AM
- The coffee being brewed
- Name tag table photos
- "The lobby at 7:25 vs. 7:45" comparison
- Sponsor shoutouts and what their support makes possible

## 3. Content Format Recommendations

### Priority Formats (ranked by Facebook algorithm favor and BOCC fit)

**1. Short-Form Video / Reels (highest priority)**
Facebook heavily favors Reels and native video. Even simple phone-shot content outperforms polished graphics.

Ideas:
- 15-30 second "vibe check" from Tuesday morning (pan across the room, coffee being poured, people talking)
- Quick attendee testimonials: "What's one thing you've gotten from BOCC?" (ask permission first)
- Time-lapse of the lobby filling up from 7:30 to 8:00
- "Gives and Asks" highlight: feature one particularly interesting give or ask (with permission)
- Walking tour: "Here's how to find us at Seneca One" -- enter building, escalator, find the group

**2. Photos with People (high priority)**
Community photos are BOCC's most shareable asset. Every Tuesday should produce at least one good group or candid shot.

Ideas:
- Candid networking shots (not posed -- people mid-conversation)
- "The Tuesday crowd" wide shot
- Name tag close-ups (with permission) showing the variety of businesses represented
- Coffee cup + conversation shots
- Before/after: empty lobby vs. full event

**3. Carousel Posts / Photo Albums**
Use albums for recap posts and multi-photo stories.

Ideas:
- Weekly recap album: 4-6 photos from Tuesday
- "Faces of BOCC" monthly album
- Afternoon Edition venue photos
- Year-in-review photo collections

**4. Text-Based Story Posts**
Simple text posts with a personal, narrative tone can perform well when they tell a real story.

Ideas:
- "I started BOCC because..." origin story from Zack
- "This morning, someone came up to me after Gives and Asks and said..."
- Reflection posts: "Three years ago, we had 10 people on a Tuesday. This week we had 32."

**5. Facebook Events (see Section 4)**

**6. Polls and Questions**
Use sparingly but strategically to drive engagement.

Ideas:
- "What time works best for Afternoon Edition?" (poll)
- "What's one thing you wish you knew before starting your business?"
- "Coffee or tea at BOCC?" (lighthearted engagement bait)
- "What neighborhoods should we visit for Afternoon Edition?"

**7. Link Posts (lowest organic reach -- use sparingly)**
Links to 716coffee.club, Eventbrite, or 716.social will get the least organic reach. Reserve link posts for when the link itself is the value (new blog post, registration for a special event). When you do share a link, put the context and value in the post text itself, not just "check this out."

### Format Mix Per Week

| Format | Frequency | Day |
|--------|-----------|-----|
| Photo post (Tuesday recap or candid) | 1x/week | Tuesday or Wednesday |
| Video/Reel | 1x/week | Any day |
| Text story or community spotlight | 1x/week | Thursday or Friday |
| Event share/reminder | 1x/week | Monday |
| Poll, question, or ecosystem share | 1x every 2 weeks | Varies |

Facebook Events are the single most important feature for BOCC on this platform. Local event discovery is one of the few remaining areas where Facebook has no real competitor.

### Recurring Weekly Event

Create a **recurring weekly event** for BOCC:

- **Title:** Buffalo Open Coffee Club -- Free Tuesday Networking
- **Frequency:** Every Tuesday
- **Time:** 7:30 AM - 9:00 AM
- **Location:** Seneca One Tower, 1 Seneca St, Buffalo, NY 14203
- **Category:** Networking
- **Description:**

> Free weekly networking for Buffalo's entrepreneurs, founders, small business owners, freelancers, and students.
>
> 7:30 AM -- Open networking. Grab free coffee or tea, pick up a name tag, and start meeting people.
> 8:10 AM -- Gives and Asks. Share what you need help with or what you can offer. Quick (20-30 sec), optional, and never a sales pitch.
> 8:30 AM -- No hard out. Co-work, keep talking, or head to the office.
>
> No cost. No membership. No registration required (but RSVPs help us plan for coffee).
>
> Parking: Street parking is free before 9 AM. Metro Rail stops at Seneca One.
>
> First time? Just show up. Say it's your first time during Gives and Asks and the room will welcome you.
>
> Organized by BootSector 501(c)(3) | 716coffee.club

- **Cover Image:** Use a crowd shot from BOCC, not the logo. Events with people photos get significantly more clicks.
- **Co-hosts:** Add Zack Glick's personal profile and the BootSector page (if one exists) as co-hosts. This extends reach to their networks.

### Afternoon Edition Events

Create individual events for each Afternoon Edition date:

| Date | Event Title | Location |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Fri March 27 | BOCC Afternoon Edition at Filled with Love Buffalo Cafe | Filled with Love Buffalo Cafe |
| Wed April 15 | BOCC Afternoon Edition at Fitz Books & Waffles | Fitz Books & Waffles |
| Thu May 14 | BOCC Afternoon Edition at Meet & Eat Charcuterie | Meet & Eat Charcuterie |

For each Afternoon Edition event:
- Tag the venue's Facebook page as co-host or in the description
- Use a photo of the venue if possible
- Cross-post to the venue's page if they allow it

### Event Promotion Cadence

For each weekly BOCC event:
- **Monday, 5-7 PM:** Share the event to the page feed with a personal note ("See you tomorrow morning...")
- **Tuesday, 7:15 AM:** Optional quick "We're here!" Story or post

For Afternoon Edition events:
- **2 weeks before:** Create and share the event
- **1 week before:** Reminder post with venue details
- **Day before:** Final reminder
- **Day of:** "Heading to [venue] in a few hours" post
- **Day after:** Recap with photos

### Why This Matters

Facebook Events appear in:
- Local event discovery feeds
- "Events near me" searches
- Friends' event calendars when someone marks "Interested" or "Going"
- Notification reminders to attendees

Each person who clicks "Interested" broadcasts the event to their network. This is free, organic reach that no other post type can match for a local event.

## 5. Posting Frequency and Cadence

### Recommended Schedule: 3-4 Posts Per Week

For a community page of BOCC's size, consistency matters more than volume. Three to four posts per week is sustainable and avoids audience fatigue.

| Day | Post Type | Notes |
|-----|-----------|-------|
| **Monday** | Tuesday Preview / Event Share | "Tomorrow morning" reminder. Share the recurring event. Include a reason to come this specific week if possible. |
| **Tuesday** | Live Content (optional) | Quick photo, Story, or Reel from the event itself. Not required every week but valuable when done. |
| **Wednesday** | Tuesday Recap | Photos from the event, attendee count, highlight from Gives and Asks, or a community spotlight. This is your anchor post each week. |
| **Thursday or Friday** | Community/Ecosystem/Spotlight | Rotate between attendee features, testimonials, Buffalo ecosystem shares, behind-the-scenes, or Afternoon Edition promotion. |

### Timing

- **Weekday posts:** 11:30 AM - 1:00 PM (lunch break browsing) or 5:00 - 7:00 PM (evening scrolling)
- **Monday event reminder:** 5:00 - 7:00 PM (so it is fresh for Tuesday morning)
- **Tuesday live content:** During the event (7:30 - 9:00 AM)
- **Wednesday recap:** 12:00 PM (maximum reach for a "what you missed" post)

### Stories

Use Facebook Stories 2-3 times per week:
- Tuesday morning: behind-the-scenes, coffee setup, crowd arriving
- Afternoon Edition events
- Quick polls or questions

Stories have lower reach than feed posts but build familiarity with existing followers and keep the page looking active.

## 6. Facebook Group Opportunity Assessment

### Recommendation: Do NOT create a Facebook Group at this time.

**Reasoning:**

BOCC already has an online community platform at 716.social. Creating a Facebook Group would:
- Split community conversation across two platforms
- Create moderation overhead that a volunteer-run organization cannot sustain
- Compete with 716.social for engagement and member attention
- Risk becoming a spam magnet without active daily moderation

**When to reconsider:**
- If 716.social adoption stalls and the community clearly prefers Facebook
- If BOCC grows past 80-100 weekly attendees and needs a larger online funnel
- If a dedicated volunteer steps up to moderate a Facebook Group daily

**Alternative approach:**
- Use the Facebook Page itself for community engagement (comments, polls, questions)
- Direct community conversation to 716.social with regular mentions in posts
- Join and participate in existing Buffalo-area Facebook Groups (see Section 10) rather than creating a competing one

### Existing Group Strategy

Instead of creating a BOCC group, Zack or a BOCC volunteer should actively participate in these existing Buffalo Facebook Groups:

- Buffalo NY Entrepreneurs / Small Business Owners (or similar)
- Buffalo Networking groups
- Buffalo Young Professionals
- WNY Small Business groups
- University at Buffalo / Buffalo State / Canisius alumni groups
- Neighborhood-specific groups (Elmwood Village, North Buffalo, etc.)

Rules of engagement for existing groups:
- Contribute value first (answer questions, share resources)
- Share BOCC events only when the group rules permit event promotion
- Never spam -- one BOCC mention per month per group maximum
- Always frame it as an invitation, not an advertisement

## 7. Community Engagement Tactics

### On-Page Engagement

**Respond to every comment within 4 hours.** For a community page, responsiveness signals that real humans are behind it. Even a simple "Thanks for coming!" or "See you next Tuesday!" builds connection.

**Ask questions in post captions.** End every post with a question or prompt:
- "What was your favorite conversation this morning?"
- "Who are you bringing next week?"
- "Drop a comment if this is the week you finally make it to BOCC"

**Tag attendees in photos (with permission).** Tagged photos appear in attendees' timelines, exposing BOCC to their entire network. This is the highest-leverage organic growth tactic on Facebook.

Before tagging, establish a simple permission system:
- At check-in, ask: "Are you okay with being tagged in photos on social media?"
- Or post photos and invite people to tag themselves: "Tag yourself if you were at BOCC this morning!"

**React to comments with the page account.** Use reactions (not just likes) on comments. The heart reaction feels more personal and community-oriented.

### User-Generated Content

Encourage attendees to:
- Check in at Seneca One Tower on Facebook when they arrive
- Share their own photos and tag @buffaloopencoffeeclub
- Post about BOCC on their personal profiles (this is more valuable than any page post)

Amplify UGC by:
- Sharing attendee posts to the BOCC page (with permission)
- Screenshotting and reposting LinkedIn testimonials to Facebook (you already have these assets: Scott, Yelenna, Lena, Jennifer, Benjamin, Freed)
- Creating a "What people are saying" carousel from multiple testimonials

### First-Timer Welcome

Create a dedicated "First Time at BOCC?" post and pin it to the top of the page. Include:
- What to expect (the 7:30/8:10/8:30 format)
- Where to park
- How to find the group inside Seneca One
- "Just show up -- no registration required"
- A welcoming photo of the community

Refresh this pinned post every 2-3 months with updated photos.

### Milestone Celebrations

Track and celebrate milestones publicly:
- "We hit 500 followers on Facebook!"
- "BOCC turned 16 years old this week"
- "Our 100th Tuesday since the reboot"
- Seasonal: "First BOCC of spring" or "Last BOCC before the holidays"

## 9. Cross-Promotion Strategy

### LinkedIn to Facebook

BOCC's LinkedIn presence (linkedin.com/showcase/buffalo-open-coffee) likely reaches a more professional, B2B audience. Use Facebook to complement LinkedIn, not duplicate it.

**What to cross-post:**
- LinkedIn testimonials reshared as Facebook image posts (screenshot the LinkedIn post, add context)
- Event announcements (adapt the tone -- LinkedIn is more professional, Facebook is more casual and community-oriented)
- Community milestone celebrations

**What NOT to cross-post:**
- Long-form LinkedIn articles (they don't perform on Facebook)
- Sponsorship asks (keep those on LinkedIn where the B2B audience is)
- Identical copy across platforms (always adapt the tone)

**Tone translation:**
- LinkedIn: "Buffalo Open Coffee Club continues to demonstrate the power of community-driven networking..."
- Facebook: "Another great Tuesday morning. 32 people, free coffee, zero sales pitches. This is BOCC."

### Instagram to Facebook

Instagram (@buffaloopencoffeeclub) is visual-first. Use the automatic cross-posting feature for Reels and Stories, but be selective.

**Cross-post from Instagram to Facebook:**
- Reels (these get boosted on both platforms)
- Photo carousels of Tuesday events
- Stories (enable automatic cross-posting)

**Do not auto-cross-post:**
- Every single Instagram post (this makes the Facebook page feel like a mirror, not a destination)
- Instagram-specific content (hashtag-heavy captions, Instagram-native features)

**Best practice:** Post natively to Facebook 2-3 times per week. Cross-post Reels and select photo content 1-2 times per week. The Facebook audience should feel like the page has its own personality.

### Driving Cross-Platform Traffic

- On Facebook: "Join the conversation on 716.social" (monthly mention)
- On Facebook: "Follow us on Instagram for more photos" (in the About section, not in every post)
- On LinkedIn: "See photos from this week's BOCC on our Facebook page" (occasional)
- On all platforms: Eventbrite link for registration

### Platform Role Definition

| Platform | Primary Role | Content Strength |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Facebook** | Local community building, event discovery, older demographic reach | Events, photos, community stories, local engagement |
| **LinkedIn** | Professional credibility, B2B sponsorship, thought leadership | Testimonials, sponsorship, professional milestones, ecosystem positioning |
| **Instagram** | Visual storytelling, younger demographic, discovery | Reels, photo carousels, Stories, venue/vibe content |
| **716.social** | Ongoing community conversation, member interaction | Discussion threads, resource sharing, event RSVPs |

## 11. Facebook Ads Recommendations

### Budget Framework

For a nonprofit community event, even $50-100/month in Facebook Ads can meaningfully extend reach. Start small, measure, and scale what works.

### Campaign 1: Event Awareness (Priority)

**Objective:** Event Responses (optimize for "Interested" clicks)
**Budget:** $30-50/month
**Audience:**
- Location: Buffalo, NY + 15-mile radius
- Age: 25-55
- Interests: Entrepreneurship, Small Business, Startups, Networking, Business Networking, Freelancing
- Exclude: Current page followers (you want new people)

**Creative:** Use the best community photo from a recent Tuesday. Real people outperform graphics. Caption:

> Free every Tuesday. 7:30 AM. Seneca One Tower lobby.
> Buffalo Open Coffee Club is where entrepreneurs, founders, and small business owners connect over free coffee. No sales pitches. No membership fees. Just community.
> Click "Interested" and we'll remind you before next Tuesday.

**Why this works:** "Interested" clicks on events are the cheapest engagement on Facebook Ads. Each click broadcasts the event to the responder's network and adds them to event notifications.

### Campaign 2: Page Followers (Secondary)

**Objective:** Page Likes
**Budget:** $20-30/month
**Audience:** Same as above, but include a Lookalike Audience based on current page followers (if you have 100+ followers)

**Creative:** Short video (15-30 seconds) showing the energy of a Tuesday morning. End card: "Buffalo Open Coffee Club. Free. Every Tuesday. Follow us for details."

### Campaign 3: Afternoon Edition Boost (As Needed)

**Objective:** Event Responses
**Budget:** $10-15 per event (3-4 times per year)
**Audience:** Narrow to the specific neighborhood of the venue + Buffalo metro
**Creative:** Photo of the venue with event details. Tag the venue.

### Campaign 4: Testimonial Retargeting (Advanced)

**Objective:** Video Views or Engagement
**Budget:** $15-20/month
**Audience:** Retarget people who engaged with the page or event but have not attended
**Creative:** Testimonial video or reshared LinkedIn testimonial screenshot. Caption: "Here's what happens when you actually show up."

### Ad Creative Guidelines

- Always use photos of real BOCC attendees (with permission), never stock photos
- Keep text short and specific -- the value proposition is simple (free, weekly, community)

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

Track these three numbers above all else:

1. **Facebook Event Responses per Week** -- This is the top-of-funnel indicator of whether Facebook is driving awareness
2. **New Attendees Who Found BOCC on Facebook** -- Add "How did you hear about us?" to check-in. This is the bottom-of-funnel truth
3. **Post Engagement Rate** -- This tells you whether your content resonates. Below 3%, rethink the content. Above 5%, you're doing well.
